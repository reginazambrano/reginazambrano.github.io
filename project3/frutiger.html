FRUTIGER TYPE SPECIMEN BOOK

The purpose of this project was to create a type specimen book that tells the story of the typeface Frutiger. Components of this process included research, analysis, concept development, and learning to use hierarchy and grid structure.

This project was created in Adobe InDesign and printed on cotton paper from Neenah. Special components include: black light features, holographic symbols and transparency page.

MOOD BOARDS

These mood boards were created based on the research conducted on Frutiger. Inspired by the uses of Frutiger, I searched for images that would inspire my future composition.

CONCEPT DEVELOPMENT

I was originally inclined to create a book similar to that of a Swiss passport due to the use of Frutiger in it. However, I was further inspired by the use of the typeface on the Euro banknotes. Therefore, in order to convey the visual representation of them, I decided to take the bank note’s features apart and make a booklet inspired in them.

In order to go forward with this idea, the components I decided to implement onto the booklet were: holographic symbols, transparency, and black light features, besides the obvious visual components of color, shape, and paper type.

PROCESS

With an idea in mind, now it was time to go through and create different versions of the intended booklet, and improve on those. Going through different versions and feedback helped form the best version of the booklet. Major improvements were made in terms of grid structure and shape element repetition.
